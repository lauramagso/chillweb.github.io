About CHILL

CHILL is a local community group in Carrickfergus.
OUR AIM is to improve and promote positive mental health and wellbeing, to raise awareness of suicide prevention in the Mid & East Antrim Borough Council areas.

Maggie Laverty

Clinical Lead and Special Advisor

Maggie has been qualified many years as a counsellor and has extensive experience in all aspects of mental health. Her passion is working with children and she is a dedicated advocate for suicide prevention. Maggie is a founder member of CHILL and the “Go To” person for advice and assistance.

Andrea Ellis

Committee Member & Complementary Therapist

Andrea got involved with CHILL due to her passion for Complementary therapies. She is a member of “The Federation of Holistic Therapists” and loves to help people make changes to their lives. Andreas own lifetime experiences and the 20 years of training and further education stands her in good stead when dealing with others.

Chris Dorrian

Committee Member and CHILL walking group Lead

Chris Dorrian is a local man who has lived in Carrickfergus all his life, after doing the “Great North Run” to raise funds for mental health he joined CHILL in 2020. In 2022 he took over the walking group, completed the walk team training and developed the group to over 30 participants which has been an excellent achievement. His passion like others is to help the local community in Mid and East Antrim.

Eleanor Ellerslie

Committee member and Bereavement Counsellor

Eleanor works for CRUSE Bereavement support, offering people help at the most challenging time of their life after the loss of someone close. At a local level Eleanor facilitates a group in Carrickfergus “Bereaved by suicide” alongside the Northern Trust, she is also an active committee member in CHILL.

Brian Smyth

Committee member & Emergency Services Liaison

Brian is a serving member of NIFRS based in Carrickfergus with 34 years service. Understanding mental health and seeing first hand the devastation caused to the individual and the family circle by Post Traumatic Stress injuries prompted Brian to respond. “I wanted to be more informed to help colleagues so I undertook training so I could be more proactive than reactive in helping others.

Stephen Weir M.B.E

Chairperson - Veterans Champion

Stephen has lived in Carrickfergus all his life and has a keen interest in community issues and mental health. He is a diligent advocate for the Youth in the Borough.

Bobby Haddn

Independent Councillor for te Kockagh Ward

Bobby has lived in Greenisland/Carrickfergus all of his life and he is a Founder member and Trustee of Greenisland football club. Bobby has recently retired from a distinguished career in N.Ireland Civil Service and is now working part time as a drugs and alcohol Counsellor. Bobby is passionate about football and developing opportunities for the local youth through sport.

Marie Anne Templeton

Secretary and Menopause Café Lead Facilitator

Marie Ann has worked in the community sector in Belfast for over 15 years and her passion is community development. Marie Anne brings a wealth of knowledge and experience to the CHILL team and her hard work and dedication as the secretary on a voluntary basis is outstanding. One of her latest projects is the menopause café which has been very successful in the early stages and undoubtedly will go from strength to strength.

Darren Wren

Trasurer an Sea Swim Co-ordinator

Darren was a train driver for 33 years and during that time he supported and assisted colleagues through traumatic events. He is a great asset to CHILL in his role as treasurer and his keen interest in mental health is complimented by the training he has undertaken as a volunteer.
